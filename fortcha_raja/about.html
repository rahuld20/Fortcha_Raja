About the Mandal

The story, history, people, festival, seva and heritage of Fortcha Raja — the beloved Ganpati of Fort, South Mumbai.

Who we are History Karyakari Mandal Festival & Theme Social Activities Transparency

We, the Fort Cha Raja Sarvajanik Utsav Mandal, were established in 1962 with a vision rooted in strong values, ethics and social responsibility. Inspired by the ideals of Lokmanya Bal Gangadhar Tilak, our Mandal has continued to uphold the rich cultural traditions of Ganeshotsav while contributing positively to society.

Who we are

Today, we proudly stand as a registered Mandal with over 65 years of dedicated service and celebration.

For more than five decades, we have celebrated Ganeshotsav with immense devotion, enthusiasm and grandeur. Along with preserving our cultural heritage, we have consistently promoted meaningful social causes — including spreading awareness through the message ‘Save Environment, Save Animals’.

Our objective

Our primary objective is to use the celebration of Ganeshotsav as a platform to inspire social awareness, unity and community welfare.

What we do

Beyond the days of the utsav, the mandal supports blood-donation drives, medical camps, educational help and seasonal relief. See our social activities and fund utilisation below.

The continued success and growth of our Mandal would not have been possible without the generous financial support, encouragement and creative contributions of our esteemed supporters and organisations. To all of them, we are deeply grateful.

1962

The beginning

In 1962, inspired by the ideals of Lokmanya Tilak, the residents and traders of Fort establish the Fort Cha Raja Sarvajanik Utsav Mandal — rooted in values, ethics and social responsibility.

Trust registered

The mandal is formally registered as a public charitable trust (Reg. No.).

Decades on

A South Mumbai favourite

Fortcha Raja becomes one of the most loved darshans of the Fort area, known for its grace and the warmth of its volunteers.

Tradition

The heritage visarjan

Our immersion procession through Fort's colonial lanes — past Horniman Circle and Ballard Estate to Girgaon Chowpatty — becomes a sight of its own.
